About The Light Box

Understanding Color and Thermal Energy

Project Overview

The Light Box is a comprehensive STEAM project designed to explore the relationship between color and thermal energy absorption. This investigation combines scientific research, data analysis, and visual communication to answer a critical question about how different colors interact with light energy.

The Motivation

Understanding how different colors absorb thermal energy has practical applications in various fields, from architecture and climate control to fashion and environmental science. By systematically testing different colored surfaces under controlled conditions, we can gather empirical data that reveals the physics behind color absorption properties.

Project Goals

🎯 Primary Goal

Determine the quantitative relationship between paper color and thermal energy absorption when exposed to a constant light source.

🔍 Secondary Goal

Develop reliable experimental protocols that can be replicated and verified by other researchers.

📚 Learning Goal

Apply the scientific method while integrating technology, engineering, design, and mathematical analysis.

Hypothesis

If the paper color is darker (like black), then the temperature will increase more than lighter colors, because darker colors absorb more light energy and convert it into thermal energy.

When different colors are exposed to the same light source, darker colors absorb a wider spectrum of light compared to lighter colors. This absorbed light energy is transformed into kinetic vibration of the paper molecules, which is measured as temperature increase.

Experimental Design Highlights

  • Controlled Variables: Light intensity, distance from source, duration of exposure, sample size
  • Test Colors: White, Gray, Red, Green, and Black papers
  • Measurement Period: 5 minutes of continuous exposure
  • Temperature Monitoring: Precise degree Celsius measurements
  • Repetition: Multiple trials to ensure consistency and reliability

Background & Science

Different colors reflect and absorb different amounts of light. The darker the color is, the more light the paper absorbs. When the paper absorbs the light, the energy is transformed into kinetic vibration of the paper molecules, and this is measured as temperature. Because darker colors absorb a wider spectrum of light wavelengths, the temperature will rise more significantly compared to lighter colors. This principle is fundamental to understanding thermal properties of materials across various applications.

Key Concepts

Thermal Energy: The energy in a substance due to the kinetic energy of its particles. When light is absorbed by a material, it converts to thermal energy, causing a temperature increase.
Light Absorption: The process by which light enters a material and doesn't exit as reflected or transmitted light. The absorbed light energy is converted to other forms, primarily heat.
Color and Wavelength: Different colors correspond to different wavelengths of visible light. The color we see depends on which wavelengths are reflected; the wavelengths that are absorbed contribute to thermal energy.

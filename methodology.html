Methodology & Research

How We Conducted Our Experiment

Research Question

To what extent does the color of paper (White, Gray, Red, Green, and Black) affect the rate of thermal energy absorption, measured in degrees Celsius (°C) over 5 minutes, when held at a constant automated distance from a 60W incandescent light source?

Hypothesis

We hypothesized that darker colors would absorb more thermal energy than lighter colors when exposed to the same light source for the same duration. Specifically, we predicted the order of heat absorption from highest to lowest would be:

  1. Black
  2. Red/Green (approximately equal)
  3. Gray
  4. White

Experimental Procedure

Equipment & Materials

Light Source

60W incandescent light bulb positioned at a fixed distance to provide consistent illumination

Temperature Measurement

Calibrated digital thermometer for accurate temperature readings in degrees Celsius

Test Samples

Same-sized paper samples in five colors: White, Gray, Red, Green, and Black

Control Setup

Automated positioning system to maintain constant distance and angle for all tests

Detailed Experimental Method

  1. Setup: A 60W incandescent lamp is placed inside a dark box to control light distribution and prevent external light interference
  2. Sample Preparation: A 5cm × 5cm colored paper sample is placed 200mm away from the light source
  3. Precision Control: An ultrasonic distance sensor measures the distance between the paper and the light bulb. A motor with a lead screw mechanism provides micro-control for precise distance maintenance across all trials
  4. Temperature Measurement Setup: An infrared camera is placed 10cm away for measuring the paper temperature. An additional IR sensor is used to prevent anomalies. Both measurements are combined and averaged for accuracy
  5. Safety Check: Place a temperature sensor to monitor for overheating and initiate emergency stop if needed. Cover the box with heat-proof materials
  6. Initialization: After verifying all equipment operates correctly, close the box lid and initiate the data collection sequence
  7. Monitoring: Monitor the situation via LCD display and verify that data is being recorded properly
  8. Data Collection: Record temperature readings for the entire 5-minute exposure period
  9. Repetition: Allow the sample to cool and repeat the entire procedure for each colored paper sample

Variables

Variable Type Description
Independent Variable (IV) Color of paper: White, Gray, Red, Green, Black (5 distinct values)
Dependent Variable (DV) Temperature increase (°C) measured with infrared temperature sensor and camera over 5 minutes

Controlled Variables

Controlled Variable Value & Units Method Why It's Important
Distance from light bulb 200 mm Ultrasonic sensor measures distance; motor with lead screw maintains constant distance Distance affects number of photons reaching the paper; longer distance = more light absorption by air/materials
Light bulb power 60W incandescent Use the same light bulb for all trials Different wattages produce different light intensities and heat; must remain constant
Heat of light bulb Monitored with temperature sensor Cover box with heat-proof materials; use temperature sensor for monitoring; emergency stop if overheating Excessive heat could burn equipment and affect measurement accuracy
Direction of light Perpendicular to paper surface Fixed light source position in dark box ensures consistent angle Light angle affects energy absorption; different angles = different absorption rates
Paper sample size 5cm × 5cm Cut all paper samples to identical dimensions Larger surface area = more light absorption; must be uniform for valid comparisons
Exposure time 5 minutes (300 seconds) Use timer to ensure exact duration for all trials Longer exposure = more thermal energy absorption; duration must be identical

Data Analysis Methods

  • Descriptive Statistics: Calculate mean, median, and standard deviation for each color
  • Graphical Analysis: Create line graphs showing temperature change over time for each color
  • Comparative Analysis: Use bar charts to compare final temperature increases across colors
  • Trend Analysis: Identify patterns in absorption rates for different colors

Risk Assessment & Safety

⚠️ Hot Bulb

Hazard: Bulb surface may burn skin.

Precaution: Do not touch the bulb while it is on.

🔥 Box Burning

Hazard: Heat may burn the box material.

Precaution: Control light bulb position so it doesn't touch the box. Monitor with temperature sensor.

⚡ Electrical Shock

Hazard: Mains power and Arduino wiring.

Precaution: Ensure all wires are insulated. Follow electrical safety protocols.

🔨 Glass Breaking

Hazard: Dropped bulb creates sharp fragments.

Precaution: Secure lamp to stable base. Handle with care.

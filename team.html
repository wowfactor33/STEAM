Meet Our Team

The People Behind The Light Box Project

Project Team Members

This STEAM project was developed through the collaborative efforts of four dedicated students in Class 7A under the supervision of DMR. Our team combined diverse skills in scientific research, engineering, data analysis, and communication to successfully design and execute this thermal energy absorption experiment.

👨‍💼 Wesley Song

Group Leader | Technical Lead & Engineering

Wesley served as group leader and handled all technical and engineering aspects of the project. He designed and assembled the linear motion system using the lead screw and stepper motor, programmed the Arduino microcontroller, and ensured all sensors were properly calibrated and integrated. Wesley developed this comprehensive website to communicate our findings and oversaw equipment setup, calibration, and system integration throughout the testing phase. His engineering expertise made precise distance control possible and ensured smooth operation of all automated systems.

✓ Project Leadership
✓ System Engineering
✓ Arduino Programming
✓ Sensor Calibration
✓ Web Development

👨‍🔬 Taejoon Kang

Research Lead & Data Analyst

Taejoon specialized in experimental design and statistical analysis. He developed the precise measurement protocols, ensured accurate temperature data collection, and conducted the detailed data analysis that revealed patterns in color-based heat absorption. His mathematical insights transformed raw observations into meaningful conclusions.

✓ Experimental Design

Collaboration & Teamwork

📋 Planning Phase

Together, we developed the research question, hypothesized outcomes, and meticulously planned every aspect of the experiment to ensure scientific accuracy and reproducibility.

🔬 Execution Phase

We conducted all trials with precision, recording detailed measurements and maintaining consistent experimental conditions. Regular communication ensured data quality and experimental integrity.

📊 Analysis Phase

Our analytical collaboration transformed raw data into insights, with statistical analysis confirming patterns and creating comprehensive visualizations for clear communication.

📢 Communication Phase

We worked together to present findings through this immersive website, ensuring scientific accuracy while making the content accessible and visually engaging for all audiences.

Skills & Competencies

This project demonstrates our ability to:

  • Design Controlled Experiments: Create variables, control conditions, and ensure reproducibility
  • Collect & Analyze Data: Accurately record measurements and extract meaningful patterns from data
  • Think Critically: Question assumptions, develop hypotheses, and validate conclusions with evidence
  • Communicate Scientifically: Present complex findings in clear, accessible language
  • Work Collaboratively: Combine different skill sets and perspectives to achieve common goals
  • Apply Technology: Use digital tools for measurement, analysis, visualization, and presentation

Learning Outcomes

Through this STEAM project, we gained hands-on experience in:
• Scientific method and experimental design
• Physics of light and thermal energy
• Data collection and statistical analysis
• Scientific visualization and communication
• Web development and digital presentation
• Teamwork and project management
• Critical thinking and problem-solving

Our Journey

This project represents months of planning, testing, analyzing, and refining. From our initial curiosity about why different colors feel different in the sun to our final conclusions supported by empirical data, this journey has been both challenging and rewarding. We're proud of what we've accomplished together and excited to share our findings with the wider community.

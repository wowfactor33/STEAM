The Light Box

Exploring Thermal Energy Absorption in Colors

Class 7A | Teacher: DMR | Group 2

Welcome to Our STEAM Project

Welcome to Group 2's STEAM Project Website! We are excited to share our research on how different paper colors affect thermal energy absorption when exposed to a light source.

Our experiment combines Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics to investigate the relationship between color and heat absorption. Through careful observation and data collection, we've discovered fascinating insights about thermal properties.

🔬 Scientific Method

We followed rigorous experimental protocols to ensure accurate and reproducible results in our thermal energy study.

📊 Data Analysis

Comprehensive data visualization helps us understand the patterns and relationships in color absorption rates.

🎨 Creative Design

Our experiment combines scientific inquiry with visual observation, making color science engaging and accessible.

Quick Facts

  • Independent Variable: Paper color (White, Gray, Red, Green, Black)
  • Dependent Variable: Temperature change (°C) over 5 minutes
  • Light Source: 60W incandescent bulb at constant distance
  • Measurement Unit: Degrees Celsius (°C)
  • Team Members: Taejoon Kang, Wesley Song, Damon Kim, Andrew Park
